About Us

“Today, no walls can separate humanitarian or human rights crisis in one part of the world from national security crises in another. What begins with the failure to uphold the dignity of one life all too often ends with a calamity for entire nations.”

-Kofi Annan, Former Secretary General of the United Nations



Model United Nations, popularly referred to as MUN, is an academic simulation of the United Nations and its various organs. In this creative simulation, participants take up the role of international diplomats and representatives of countries in various UN bodies.
The motto of Techfest World Model United Nations is CONVERGE TO RESOLVE, and we aim to develop universally acceptable proposals to solve some of the pressing global issues including poverty, health, energy and education, conflict, wildlife and conservation, climate change, imbalanced trade among others. These resolutions will be forwarded to UN itself as youth opinion for active consideration for its own functioning & planning purposes.
Continuing the legacy of Techfest World MUN 2016, which saw a participation of over 520 Delegates coming together from more than 20 countries including Czech Republic, Bangladesh, Philippnes, Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, Egypt and Australia among others, with 4 school and 8 college level committees and earning a total prize worth of more than 2.6 lakh INR, we are extremely enthused to invite you to the Techfest World Model United Nations 2017.

This edition of Techfest will take place during 29-31st of December 2017. Apart from the conference, you get an amazing opportunity to celebrate New Year’s Eve here in Mumbai.

In case there are any changes in the technical specification or otherwise, we shall update you as soon as possible.

FAQ

  • What is Model United Nations?

    Model United Nations (also Model UN or MUN) is an academic simulation of the United Nations that aims to educate and encourage participants to discuss about major issues concerning the world, topics in international relations, diplomacy and the United Nations agenda.

  • What is Techfest?

    Techfest is the annual Science and Technological Festival of Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, India. It is well established today as Asia's Largest Science and Technology festival and has surpassed 20 golden years of creating an unmatched aura of a science and technology spectacle year after year. Techfest 2016 saw a footfall of more than 160,000 students.
    For more info : www.techfest.org

  • How can I contact the organizing team of TWMUN, IIT Bombay?

    You can email us at sachit@techfest.org

  • What are the dates of TWMUN, IIT Bombay?

    TWMUN, IIT Bombay is a three day conference and will be held during Techfest 2017-18 from 29-31st December 2017.

    All students with a valid ID card of their high school or college can register for TWMUN, IIT Bombay. However, exceptional cases are sometimes made for recent graduates.

  • When will the registration start for Delegates?

    Coming Soon.

    The dress code is traditionally western business attire for the entire duration of the conference. However, in line with the UN's conventions, Delegates may wear the national costume of the country they are representing as long as this is not seized as an opportunity to dress less formally.

    They will be uploaded to the committees page prior to the announcement of committee assignments in mid-october.

  • Why are there two topics listed for the committees?

    Delegates will spend the first session deciding and voting on which topic to discuss, and will debate that issue first. If that topic is resolved before the end of the conference, the second issue will be debated too, but this cannot be guaranteed. Delegates should prepare equally well for both topics.

  • What awards will be presented at TWMUN, IIT Bombay?

    The awards will be presented at the discretion of the chairs of each committee and will include 1 Best Delegate and 1 Honorable Mentions/High Commendations. 2 awards will be presented to the best and the second best chair respectively and 1 award to the best International Ambassador at the discretion of the Secretary General and the Organizing committee.

    Mumbai being the economic capital of India offers various modes of transport. You can find more on Wiki Travel.

  • Do I get help for my VISA application?

    If you need assistance with your Visa application, please email us a scanned copy of your passport. Thus, we recommend that you register to the conference and notify us that you require a Visa as early as possible.

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

The United Nations Security Council is one of the six principal organs and the primary decision making body of the United Nations. In effect since 1946, the UNSC undertakes matters of peacekeeping and security, rules out international sanctions and is responsible for new membership to the UN and amendments to the existing UN Charter. The UNSC typically consists of 15 member nations, of which 5 - USSR (now Russian Federation), United Kingdom, France, United States of America and China (now People’s Republic of China) - are permanent members with veto powers. The remaining 10 members are elected on a biennial basis with the presidency of the SC switching between its members on a monthly basis.

The agendas for the UNSC at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Constructive Measures to Avoid Imminent Armed Conflicts Owing to Paucity of Fresh Water in South Asia
  • 2). Discussion on the Yemen Civil War and the role religious rebel outfits play in propagating violence in conflict zones

United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC)

The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime, originally the Office for Drug Control and Crime Prevention is part of the United Nations Development Program. It was formed as a conglomerate of the United Nations International Drug Control Program and the Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice Division in 1997. The UNODC seeks to aid the UN in addressing matters of illicit drug trade and associated criminal activity, prevention of drug abuse, human trafficking, money laundering and international terrorism.

The agendas for the UNODC at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Combating Drug Trafficking in the Americas
  • 2). Establishing a comprehensive framework that standardises the prescription, sale and usage of recreational drugs

Continuous Crisis Committee (CCC)

Continuous Crisis Committee

At TWMUN 2017, the Continuous Crisis Committee is our flagship committee, conceptualised to allow free flowing and eloquent debate on a versatile agenda. With sustainability as a theme, the topics for debate will remain dynamic over the entire duration of committee sessions - there is no fixed agenda, per se.

This MUN classic format is meant to challenge seasoned MUNners and debaters and enthral audience with the unparalleled level of debate.

International Maritime Organization (IMO)

Consisting of five main committees and supported by other technical subcommittees, the International Maritime Organisation is a specialised wing of the UN that is responsible for the regulation of shipping worldwide with emphasis on environmental concerns. legal and technical matters, security and commercial shipping. Comprising 172 member states and 3 associate members, established in 1959, the IMO is headquartered in London.

The agendas for the IMO at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Improving Shipbreaking Rules and Regulations
  • 2). Improving existing norms on safety of navigation, communication, search and rescue operations with special emphasis on naval drills in the high seas

United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

Founded in 1972 as an outcome of the United Nations Conference on the Human Environment, the United Nations Environment Programme is the apex body within the United Nations responsible for aiding member nations in devising sound environmental policies for effective resource management. Some pertinent issues addressed by the UNEP include the issues of rampant climate change and imminent desertification in landlocked areas. It also addresses concerns regarding ecosystems - both marine and terrestrial.

The agendas for the UNEP at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Incorporating Renewable Energy Sources into the Energy Mix of Sub Saharan Countries
  • 2). Adapting the Sustainable Development Goals to target sustainable and equitable economies in conjunction with the 2030 agenda

Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC)

Comprising of 45 member states and 13 associate members, the United Nations Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean is a regional conglomerate aimed at improving means of economic cooperation in Latin America and the Caribbean. Headquartered in Santiago, Chile, it reports directly to the Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) of the UN.

The agendas for the ECLAC at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Solutions for Recessions in Major Economies in Latin America
  • 2). Constructive measures to increase Foreign Direct Investment in Latin America

UN Women

Empowerment of women in all spheres of personal life and governance is the unwritten motto of the United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and Empowerment of Women, also called UN Women. UN Women is a part of the UN Development Group and functions through a multi tiered intergovernmental governance structure that rules and enacts policy decisions. UN Women is tasked with leading, coordinating and promoting the accountability of the UN in its work on gender equality and women’s empowerment.

The agendas for the UN WOMEN at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Improving Opportunities and Uplifting Women in the Middle East
  • 2). Discussion on the role and rights of women and dependents in situations of armed conflict

Disarmament and International Security(DISEC)

The United Nations’ General Assembly’s First Committee on Disarmament and International Security is one of six main committees of the UNGA. As the name suggests, this committee discusses issues concerning disarmament and related international security threats. All 193 member states of the UN are mandated to attend the annual DISEC meetings held in the month of October.

The agendas for the DISEC at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Demilitarising regions of ecological importance to promote environmental sustainability
  • 2). De escalating growing tensions in the Korean Peninsula

United Nations General Assembly Third Committee (SOCHUM) - School

The United Nations’ General Assembly’s Third Committee is the Social, Cultural and Humanitarian Committee that deals with matters of cultural and humanitarian relevance, this includes broad mandate discussion on the preservation of cultures and languages, the ongoing refugee crisis and other humanitarian issues.

The agendas for the SOCHUM at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Comprehensive Solutions to Protect Cultures and Languages of Indigenous Tribes in South East Asia
  • 2). Discussion on extrajudicial executions, the right to peace and defining basic freedoms

World Health Organization (WHO)- School

The World Health Organisation is a specialised agency of the United Nations that is concerned with international public health. Issues related to the prevention of outbreak of epidemics, the control of the transmission of communicable diseases, regulation of medical drug use, nutrition and food security are dealt with at the WHO. Specialised cases involving the eradication of smallpox, transmission of HIV/AIDS, Ebola, malaria, Zika virus are also focussed upon at this agency.

The agendas for the WHO at Techfest World MUN 2017 are:

  • 1). Improving the Affordability and Accessibility of Life Saving Drugs
  • 2). Discussion on building capacity to advocate for improved school health programmes.

Letter from Secretary General

Dear Delegates,


It gives me immense pleasure in welcoming you all to Techfest World Model United Nations, hosted at the Indian Institute of Technology, Mumbai. This landmark Conference is a unique platform that serves as the bridge between India and the world - one of the largest Model UN Conferences in the country by participation, our numerous international collaborations and our creatively drafted agendas that touch upon pertinent issues demanding urgent discussion ensure that we don’t just invoke international diplomacy, we guarantee it.
With the two fold privilege of hosting this Conference at the Indian Institute of Technology - one of the most renowned educational institutions across the globe, which has continually promoted the importance of science and entrepreneurship in a young country like ours; and in the city of Mumbai - the gateway of India to the world, with a spirit so unique that it celebrates global cultures and a strong sense of individualism simultaneously, we assure you that the three days of this Conference will engage you in exhilarating debate, thought provoking logical arguments, an enhanced exposure to global cultures and a deep rooted realisation of the complexities of the world we inhabit and the dire need for positive change.
Former Secretary General of the United Nations, Ban Ki-moon once said, “Saving our planet, lifting people out of poverty, advancing economic growth - these are one and the same fight. We must connect the dots between climate change, water scarcity, energy shortages, global health, food security and women's empowerment. Solutions to one problem must be solutions for all.” Bearing this in mind, our Secretariat has drafted a comprehensive set of debatable topics that require dialogue, negotiation, cooperation and a unique perspective among member states for inclusive and beneficial solutions to be attained.
In the spirit of diplomacy, it is my humble recommendation that participating Delegates socialise among their peers and fellow opinionators - take the time and initiative to form human bonds that outlast the three days you will spend together - it is the thoughts you carry in your mind and the memories you carry in your heart at the end that make hosting this Conference worthwhile for us.
I look forward to the privilege of hosting you and the pleasure of meeting you soon at the IIT Mumbai Techfest World Model United Nations 2017.

Best Wishes
Rini Antony
Secretary-General
TWMUN, IIT Bombay 2017

Letter from Secretary General

Dear Delegates,


It is quite an honor for me to welcome all you aspiring diplomats to Techfest World MUN 2017 hosted by the eminent Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay. Excelling at showcasing incredible quality debates year after year, the ambitious team at IIT has once again provided a level field for college and school students alike. Raising awareness and providing a platform for public speaking across such wide demographics is nothing short of a herculean task, which the secretariat tackles with finesse.
It’s a rather interesting fact that over the past 3,400 years of recorded history, humans have lived entirely in peace for 268 years of them, a meagre 8%. Conflict has always shaken the very foundations of civilization, but it begs the question, is war truly the only problem mankind faces today? Is it not worrisome that the leaders of countries with the second largest carbon emissions declare that Global Warming is but a hoax? This, in the wake of events such as the breaking of the Antarctic Iceberg, measured to be four times the size of London. Is it not thought provoking, that there are regions on this planet riddled with drugs resulting in intoxication of youth, but face scarcity of medication drugs required to save lives? Is it okay to lack access to fresh water in the 21st century?
Model United Nations Conferences provide a platform to varied age groups to assess and express their leadership, problem solving and diplomatic abilities. Regardless of the dexterity an individual possesses to solve problems, the ability to fluently communicate his or her ideas is what truly distinguishes the individual from the rest. Not only is it necessary to address large crowds and stay aware of global occurrences, it is also expected of Delegates to profess influence over the committee, testing their lobbying and team work skills to solve and address real world problems. Be it a remote location in some part of the world where development needs a stern voice for propagation, or a corporate environment where problems could be much different in scale and magnitude, effective communication is a much needed ‘real-world’ skill, that we hope is aided and bettered for Delegates over the course of the conference.
On a parting note, I would like to emphasize on the necessity of enjoying the conference, taking away ever lasting memories and comrades from the MUN. You are bound to network with some truly incredible individuals, likely to have similar interests and passions. I look forward to meeting all of you in Techfest WorldMUN 2017.

Warm Regards and Best Wishes,
Raj Khot
Secretary-General
TWMUN, IIT Bombay 2017
